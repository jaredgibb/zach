# Figma Make Build Brief: Harborlight Therapy

## Brand + Tone

- **Brand name:** Harborlight Therapy
- **Tagline:** One-on-one therapy for steadier days and stronger relationships.
- **Voice:** calm, supportive, plain language, non-judgmental, clinical but human
- **Primary goal:** get visitors to **Book a free 15-minute consult** or **Request an appointment**

## Visual Direction

- **Style:** modern, warm, minimal, high trust
- **Color palette (suggested):**
  - Deep teal for primary actions
  - Soft sand or warm off-white backgrounds
  - Muted sage accents
  - Dark charcoal text
- **Typography:**
  - Headings: clean sans (Inter, Source Sans, or similar)
  - Body: readable sans, 16–18px
- **Layout:**
  - Max content width: ~1100–1200px
  - 8pt spacing system
  - Rounded cards, subtle shadows, lots of whitespace
- **Accessibility:**
  - AA contrast
  - Clear focus states
  - Large tap targets
  - Avoid text over busy imagery

## Site Navigation

### Top nav (sticky on scroll)

### Footer

## Page 2: Services

### Page header

- **H1:** Individual therapy services
- **Subhead:** Evidence-informed, one-on-one therapy for adults. Sessions are customized, practical, and grounded in your goals.

### Service sections (3 anchors)

#### 1) Anxiety and Burnout

- **Heading:** Anxiety and burnout support
- Copy: Anxiety can show up as racing thoughts, irritability, sleep issues, or feeling constantly “on.” Burnout can feel like numbness, low motivation, or dread. We help you understand patterns and build skills that reduce distress in daily life.
- What we may work on:
  - Nervous system regulation and coping strategies
  - Reducing avoidance and rumination
  - Sleep routines and stress boundaries
  - Confidence and decision-making
- CTA: **Request an Appointment**

#### 2) Relationship Communication

- **Heading:** Relationship communication and boundaries
- Copy: You do not need to be in couples therapy to work on relationship issues. Individual therapy can help you communicate more clearly, set boundaries, and respond differently in conflict.
- What we may work on:
  - Boundary setting without guilt
  - Patterns like people-pleasing or shutdown
  - Co-parenting communication
  - Repair after conflict
- CTA: **Talk to a Therapist**

#### 3) Grief and Life Transitions

- **Heading:** Grief, loss, and major transitions
- Copy: Grief is not linear. Change can also bring uncertainty, identity shifts, or isolation. Therapy can provide a steady place to process, remember, and rebuild.
- What we may work on:
  - Making space for grief without getting stuck
  - Meaning-making and values-based choices
  - Transition support (divorce, moves, career changes)
  - Building routines and support systems
- CTA: **Book a Free Consult**
